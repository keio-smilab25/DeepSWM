About Solar Flares

▼

Solar flares are intense bursts of electromagnetic radiation from the Sun's surface, caused by the sudden release of magnetic energy stored in the solar atmosphere. These powerful events can have significant impacts on Earth and space-based technology.

Solar flares are classified by their X-ray intensity into different classes:

  • X-class: The most intense flares that can cause planet-wide radio blackouts and long-lasting radiation storms
  • M-class: Medium-sized flares that can cause brief radio blackouts affecting polar regions
  • C-class: Small flares with few noticeable consequences on Earth
  • B and A-class (O-class): Background-level flares with minimal impact

Understanding and predicting solar flares is crucial for protecting astronauts, satellites, power grids, and communication systems from space weather effects.

About Deep Space Weather Model (DeepSWM)

▼

Deep Space Weather Model (Deep SWM) is an AI model for predicting solar flare activity, accepted at ICCV 2025.

Our model accurately and reliably predicts solar flares by capturing long-range spatio-temporal dependencies and fine-grained features in multi-channel solar image series, utilizing 672 hours (28 days) of observation data.

Key Technical Innovations:

  • Solar Spatial Encoder (SSE): Features Depth-wise Channel Selective Module and Spatio-Temporal State-Space Module for multi-wavelength analysis
  • Long-range Temporal SSM: Models temporal dependencies exceeding the solar rotation period
  • Sparse Masked Autoencoder: Novel pretraining strategy with two-phase masking to preserve crucial regions like sunspots
  • Multi-wavelength Integration: Processes ten-channel solar observations (AIA + HMI) simultaneously

Performance: Our method outperforms all baseline approaches and even surpasses human expert performance on standard metrics, representing a significant advancement in space weather prediction capabilities.
